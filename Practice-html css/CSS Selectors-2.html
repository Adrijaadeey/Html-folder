Rain is a natural phenomenon resulting from the condensation of water vapor in the atmosphere.
Clouds, which are formed by tiny water droplets or ice crystals suspended in the air, are a crucial part of this process.

When the air becomes saturated with moisture, these droplets or crystals collide and merge, growing larger and heavier.

Eventually, they fall to the ground as precipitation in the form of rain.
Rain is essential for replenishing water sources, supporting plant life, and maintaining the Earth's ecosystems.
Rainfall is essential for plants and water sources.

Clouds are collections of tiny water droplets or ice crystals

Weather patterns can be predicted by studying clouds and rain.
